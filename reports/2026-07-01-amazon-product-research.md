## 1. Top 5 Product Opportunities

### 1. Waffle Weave Cotton Kitchen Tea Towels (Set of 6)

- **Category / Subcategory:** Home & Kitchen › Kitchen & Dining › Kitchen Linen › Tea Towels
- **Why worth researching:** Tea towels are a true UK staple — daily-use, low unit price, high repeat purchase, and gift-set friendly. Waffle weave is currently on-trend for its "café aesthetic" while still being genuinely useful (absorbent, quick-dry). Sits comfortably in the £12–£22 pack range where private-label can win on presentation and cotton quality.
- **Demand signals:**
  - Kitchen linen is one of the most searched sub-categories in UK Home & Kitchen; "tea towels" is a perennial search term.
  - Steady year-round demand with a strong Q4 gifting spike.
  - Sub-£20 impulse price band → high conversion.
- **Competition signals:**
  - Crowded but highly fragmented — many generic 3rd-party sellers, few strong private-label brands with premium presentation.
  - Top listings are a mix of legacy brands (Penguin, Nicola Spring) and generic multi-packs with mediocre photography.
- **Common weaknesses in existing listings:**
  - Poor lifestyle photography — flat-lay shots on white, no kitchen scene.
  - Vague fabric spec ("100% cotton" with no GSM stated).
  - Complaints in reviews: linting on first wash, colour bleed, thin weave, sizes smaller than advertised.
  - Weak bullet points that don't sell absorbency or hang-loop utility.
- **Differentiation ideas for Rosanube:**
  - State GSM (aim ~380–450 GSM waffle), OEKO-TEX certification, pre-washed to reduce linting.
  - Sewn-in hang loop + reinforced double-stitched edges.
  - Muted "Rosanube palette" (sage, oat, terracotta, dusty blue) instead of primary colours.
  - Premium recyclable kraft packaging with a "6 towels, 6 uses" insert card.
- **Bundle / variation strategy:**
  - Core SKU: pack of 6 (2 sage / 2 oat / 2 white).
  - Variations: pack of 4 (starter) and pack of 12 (bulk / gifting).
  - Cross-bundle: 4 tea towels + 2 waffle hand towels as a "kitchen refresh" gift set.

- **Why worth researching:** Bath mats are consistent daily-use, sub-£25, and shoppers replace them every 12–18 months. The "ribbed / tufted cotton" style is on-trend as an alternative to plush shaggy mats, which get heavy negative reviews for water retention and slipping. Genuine gap for a mat that actually dries fast and stays put.
- **Demand signals:**
  - "Cotton bath mat" and "non slip bath mat" are steady, high-volume searches on Amazon UK.
  - Not seasonal; slight uptick in spring cleaning and student move-in (Aug–Sep).
  - Low return rate if fit + non-slip claim are honest.
- **Competition signals:**
  - Category dominated by generic memory foam / microfibre mats with poor durability reviews.
  - Very few "ribbed cotton + genuine anti-slip backing" premium options in mid price range.
- **Common weaknesses in existing listings:**
  - "Non-slip" claimed but backing is thin latex that peels after 5 washes — repeated review complaint.

  - Neutral palette matching towel range for cross-sell.
- **Bundle / variation strategy:**
  - Core SKU: single mat, 50×80 cm.
  - Variations: 60×90 cm, contour set (mat + pedestal).

## 2. Do Not Pursue

### A. Weighted Blankets
- **Reason:** Fails multiple rules — very heavy (expensive to ship, damages FBA margin), safety-sensitive (child suffocation warnings), and requires clear medical-adjacent claims wording. Category is also dominated by established brands (Kalmbach, Aricove, Mela) with heavy review moats.

### B. Silk Pillowcases (Mulberry Silk 22 Momme)
- **Reason:** High-return-risk category — buyers complain about colour mismatch, "not real silk" perception, and beauty-claim expectations (hair/skin) that can attract A-to-Z claims. Dominated by strong brands (Slip, Blissy, Drowsy) with premium price anchoring; a new private label would compete on price and erode margin. Also authenticity certifications add complexity.

### C. Electric Heated Throws / Blankets
- **Reason:** Excluded by config — electronics, safety certification (UKCA/CE), higher return rate, potential fire-safety liability. Not a fit for a textile-only private label.
