### 1) Anti-Slip Chenille Bath Mat (Alfombra de Baño Chenilla Antideslizante)

- **Spanish keywords:** *alfombra baño antideslizante*, *alfombrilla baño chenilla*, *alfombra ducha absorbente*
- **Category / Subcategory:** Hogar y cocina › Baño › Alfombrillas y alfombras de baño
- **Why worth researching:** Bath mats are a true replenishment SKU (wear out, get mouldy, get replaced). Chenille shag is a growing aesthetic on Amazon.es because it photographs as "soft/premium" while being cheap to source. Fits Rosanube's clean/practical brand and is easy to differentiate on colour palette and non-slip backing quality.
- **Demand signals (qualitative — to be verified in Helium 10):** Persistent presence in Baño bestsellers; broad long-tail keywords (colour + size); year-round demand with a soft peak around back-to-school / house-move season (Sep–Oct) and spring cleaning (Mar–Apr).
- **Competition signals:** Fragmented — many small brands with 100–3,000 reviews. Main leaders are generic CN private labels; few strong EU household brands. Common price band **€12–€22** for a single 50×80 cm mat.
- **Common weaknesses in existing listings:**
  - No stated GSM / pile height / backing type.
  - Complaints about the non-slip backing peeling or leaving marks on tile.
  - "Machine washable" claimed but reviews report matting after 2–3 washes.
  - Colours photographed under yellow light — real product looks different.
  - Bullets machine-translated, dimensions sometimes given only in inches.
- **Differentiation ideas for Rosanube:**
  - Publish real pile height (target ~3 cm) and GSM (target ≥1,600 g/m² for chenille shag).
  - Latex-dot backing (not a single rubber sheet, which peels) — call it out with a close-up photo.
  - Muted, "clean Iberian home" palette (arena, salvia, off-white, terracotta) — not saturated pinks/blues.
  - Real bathroom lifestyle photography on Spanish tile (Zellige / hidráulico).
  - Care label + Spanish fibre composition + wash instructions in Spanish.
- **Suggested bundle / variation strategy:** Launch **one SKU** = 50×80 cm in 3 neutral colours. Later add a 2-pack (mat + contour piece) and a 40×60 cm secondary size. **Avoid** the 3-piece toilet-lid set (higher return risk, more compliance labels).
- **Estimated risk level:** **Medium** (heavier item = higher FBA fee; backing quality drives returns).

  - Competition: 6/10
  - Differentiation: 7/10

### 2) Quilted Waterproof Pillow Protector 2-Pack (Funda Protectora de Almohada Impermeable, 50×70 cm)

- **Spanish keywords:** *funda almohada impermeable*, *protector almohada acolchado*, *funda protectora almohada 50x70*
- **Category / Subcategory:** Hogar y cocina › Ropa de cama y almohadas › Fundas de almohada › Protectores
- **Why worth researching:** Protectors are pure replenishment (sweat, drool, kids) and are almost never returned when sizing is correct — the standard Spanish pillow is **50×70 cm**, so **one size** covers the majority of the market. Very good FBA economics: light, flat-pack, small carton, low storage cost.
- **Demand signals (qualitative — to be verified):** Deep long-tail Spanish keywords; frequent presence in Ropa de cama subcategory best-sellers; steady 12-month demand with a mild lift in Jan (post-Reyes home refresh) and Sep (back-to-school + new mattress season).
- **Competition signals:** Moderate. A handful of Spanish/EU brands (Pikolin Home, Belnou, Velfont) dominate the top, but there is a wide mid-tier of 4-star listings with 200–2,000 reviews at €10–€18 for a 2-pack — beatable with better copy and packaging.
- **Common weaknesses in existing listings:**
  - "Impermeable" claimed but reviews complain of crinkly TPU noise under the ear.
  - Poor fit on **thicker** pillows (many are cut for slim pillows only).
  - No stated GSM of the top fabric, no fibre composition on the listing (Amazon.es actually requires it under EU 1007/2011).
  - Bullets in English; images are stock renders, not real photos.
  - Complaints about zip breaking after 5–10 washes.
- **Differentiation ideas for Rosanube:**
  - **Quiet TPU** membrane behind a brushed micro-polyester top (state dB claim only if tested — else drop it).
  - **Deep gusset** to fit pillows up to ~12 cm loft — this is the single most common complaint.
  - YKK-equivalent metal zip with fabric flap.
  - 50×70 cm as the launch SKU, 45×75 cm (Portuguese standard) as a fast-follow — same supplier, same packaging design.
  - Spanish-first listing, real bedroom photo, care label with fibre composition.
- **Suggested bundle / variation strategy:** Launch **2-pack, 50×70 cm, white**. Add a 4-pack for family buyers, then a 45×75 cm (PT standard) as a fast-follow SKU sharing supplier tooling. Avoid coloured versions (protector goes under the funda, colour is invisible → wasted SKU complexity).
- **Estimated risk level:** **Low–Medium** (main risk = TPU noise complaints; sample check mandatory).
- **Score /50:**
  - Demand: 8/10
  - Competition: 6/10
  - Differentiation: 7/10
  - Margin potential: 8/10
  - Risk: 8/10

### 4) Microfibre Cleaning Cloths 12-Pack, Colour-Coded (Bayetas Microfibra 12 uds)

- **Spanish keywords:** *bayetas microfibra*, *paños microfibra limpieza*, *bayetas colores*
- **Category / Subcategory:** Hogar y cocina › Productos de limpieza y para el hogar › Bayetas, paños y esponjas
- **Why worth researching:** Highest-frequency replenishment in the whole textile universe — used up and thrown away in weeks. Extremely FBA-friendly (light, flat, small pack). Colour-coded (kitchen/bath/glass/dust) is the standard differentiator; the space is still full of unbranded 10-packs with English copy.
- **Demand signals (qualitative — to be verified):** Very deep search volume on *bayetas microfibra*; year-round demand; strong sub-€15 add-on-buy pattern; frequently bundled with cleaning products.
- **Competition signals:** Fragmented but **price-competitive**. Vileda / Spontex dominate the branded side; below them, dozens of unbranded 8–12 packs at **€6–€14**. The **€15–€20 branded 12-pack with a real story** slot is thinner and beatable.
- **Common weaknesses in existing listings:**
  - No stated GSM (should be 300–400 g/m² for good cloths).
  - Colours not consistent between batches — reviews complain.
  - "Machine washable at 60 °C" claimed but reviews report edges fraying at 40 °C.
  - Packaging is a plastic bag with a paper insert — not giftable, not shelf-ready.
  - Colour-coding is left to the buyer to figure out (no printed key on packaging).
- **Differentiation ideas for Rosanube:**
  - Printed colour-code key on the sleeve (e.g. amarillo = cocina, azul = cristal, rosa = baño, verde = polvo).
  - Stated GSM ≥ 300 g/m² and 80/20 polyester–polyamide split (industry standard for pickup).
  - Overlocked edges (not glued) — call it out with a close-up.
  - Recycled kraft sleeve instead of polybag (also helps EPR packaging story in Spain).
  - Spanish-first listing with wash instructions and lint-free glass claim (only if sample proves it).
- **Suggested bundle / variation strategy:** Launch **12-pack colour-coded**. Add a **24-pack family size** and a **6-pack "cristal + coche"** microfibre-glass variant sharing the same supplier. Avoid making single-colour packs — kills the colour-code story.
- **Estimated risk level:** **Low** (light, flat, no size complexity, low return risk, minimal compliance).
