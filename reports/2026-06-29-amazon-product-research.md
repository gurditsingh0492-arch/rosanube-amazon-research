## Market notes for the day

UK home textile demand on Amazon continues to skew toward the
"quiet luxury / Scandi / washed linen" aesthetic, with shoppers
willing to pay a premium when listings clearly communicate
fabric weight (GSM), fibre origin, OEKO-TEX certification, and
muted, photo-styled colour palettes. The biggest weakness
across the category remains the same as previous research
windows: generic listings with cheap polyester pretending to
be linen, no GSM data, mismatched sizing, and unstyled product
photography. That is the gap Rosanube can credibly attack.

### 2. Turkish Cotton Peshtemal / Hammam Bath Towels

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Amazon category** | Home & Kitchen → Bathroom → Bath Linens → Bath Towels |
| **Risk level** | Low–Medium |
| **Score** | **34 / 50** |

**Why worth researching**
Lightweight, flat-woven Turkish towels (peshtemal / hammam)
continue to grow in the UK as a dual-purpose product — bath,
gym, travel, beach, picnic. They are easy and cheap to ship
(low volumetric weight), photograph beautifully, and have a
clear story (Anatolian weaving heritage). They sit in a
quieter niche than terry bath towels, which are dominated by
Christy and supermarket private label.

**Demand signals**
- Growing search volume for *"Turkish towel"*, *"hammam
  towel"*, *"lightweight bath towel"*.
- Strong cross-use (yoga / gym / travel) increases addressable
  audience beyond bathroom-only shoppers.

**Competition signals**
- A few niche brands present but no clear UK Amazon category
  king at the premium-but-affordable tier.
- Many listings are dropshipped, with thin product detail and
  weak photography.

**Common weaknesses in existing listings**
- Listings rarely state GSM or yarn count.
- Generic flat-lay photos, no in-use shots.
- Inconsistent sizing: "beach" vs "bath" sizing not clarified.
- Fringe quality is a recurring complaint in reviews (unravels,
  tangles in the wash).

**Differentiation ideas for Rosanube**
- 100% Turkish cotton, named GSM (e.g. 320 GSM), pre-washed
  for softness on first use.
- Reinforced hand-knotted fringe to address the #1 review
  complaint.
- Dual-texture (terry loops on one side, flat weave on the
  other) — practical positioning gap.
- Sensible UK colour range (sage, terracotta, charcoal, oat).
- Compact recycled-card gift sleeve, no plastic.

**Bundle / variation strategy**
- Hero: single peshtemal in 6 colours, 90 × 170 cm.
- Variant: 2-pack "his + hers" or "bath + beach".
- Cross-bundle: peshtemal + matching hand towel duo.

**Scoring**
- Demand: **7 / 10**
- Competition: **6 / 10**
- Differentiation: **7 / 10**
- Margin potential: **7 / 10**

### 3. Stonewashed 100% Linen Table Runner

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Amazon category** | Home & Kitchen → Kitchen & Dining → Tablecloths, Runners & Skirts → Table Runners |
| **Risk level** | Low |
| **Score** | **38 / 50** |

**Why worth researching**
The "tablescape" trend (Instagram / Pinterest table-setting)
keeps table linen demand high all year, with predictable
spikes around Easter, summer entertaining, and Q4 holidays.
The Amazon UK runner category is overwhelmingly polyester or
"linen-look" — a real 100% washed linen runner with clear
sizing and a premium finish is a defensible private-label
position.

**Demand signals**
- Steady year-round queries with strong holiday spikes.
- High attach-rate to other table linens (napkins, placemats,
  tablecloths) — supports basket-size growth.
- Gifting potential (housewarming, wedding registry).

**Competition signals**
- Fragmented, mostly low-quality generic sellers.
- No clear category leader in the *premium linen runner* tier

**Common weaknesses in existing listings**
- Polyester sold under "linen" keywords.

- Three lengths: 200 / 250 / 300 cm to cover most UK dining

  template), with scale shots.

**Bundle / variation strategy**
- Hero SKU: 200 cm in 5 neutrals.
- Variant SKUs: 250 cm, 300 cm.
- Cross-bundle: runner + 6 matching napkins as a "Sunday
  Table" set (very natural pairing with #5).

## Do Not Pursue (3 rejected ideas)

### A. Diatomite / diatomaceous stone bath mats
- **Why rejected:** Fragile (crack and chip in transit and in
  use), heavy for their footprint, and consistently
  high-return — exactly the inverse of the *lightweight,
  easy-to-ship, low-return* profile in `research_config.md`.
  Also outside Rosanube's "textile" brand story.

### B. Weighted blankets
- **Why rejected:** Heavy and oversized (high shipping and
  FBA storage cost), dominated by established UK brands
  (Mela, Kally Sleep, Aeyla) with strong reviews and ad spend,
  and carry quasi-therapeutic claim risk. Fails the "avoid
  big-brand dominance", "avoid oversized / heavy", and
  "avoid legal-claim uncertainty" rules.

### C. Memory-foam or latex pillows
- **Why rejected:** UK fire-safety regulations (BS 7177 / fire
  retardancy) introduce certification complexity, fit /
  firmness expectation mismatches drive high return rates, and
  the category is saturated by large dedicated brands (Silentnight,
  Panda, Simba). Fails the "no complicated certification" and
  "no big-brand dominance" rules.
